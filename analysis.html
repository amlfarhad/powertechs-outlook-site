Worker Profiles

6 Archetypes

Real worker profiles with transferable skills and habits they must unlearn.

Military / Defense

Military Logistics Specialist

Army 92A, Marine Logistics, Navy Seabee. Experienced with convoy ops, supply chains, heavy equipment in hostile environments.

  • Managing multi-vehicle convoys and equipment
  • Operating heavy machinery in field conditions
  • Maintaining detailed records and documentation
Energy — Downstream

Oil Refinery Process Operator

Process Operator, Board Operator, Field Operator. 24/7 shift work managing high-voltage systems and hazardous materials.

  • Monitoring process control systems (DCS/SCADA)
  • High-voltage electrical system awareness
  • Hazardous materials handling per OSHA
Mining

Underground Coal Miner

Continuous Mining Machine Operator, Roof Bolter, Shuttle Car Operator. Decades in confined, high-risk environments.

  • Operating heavy machinery in confined spaces
  • Strict safety protocol adherence (MSHA)
  • Electrical troubleshooting underground
Energy — Midstream

Pipeline Welder

Pipe Fitter/Welder specialist with API 1104 certification. Remote, travel-heavy work across pipeline corridors.

  • Precision welding (SMAW, GTAW, FCAW)
  • X-ray quality welds to pipeline codes
  • Heavy equipment operation (sidebooms)
Commercial Real Estate

Building Maintenance Technician

General maintenance in commercial/office buildings — HVAC, basic electrical, plumbing. As office vacancy rises with remote work, these workers need new paths.

  • Basic electrical troubleshooting (120V/208V)
  • HVAC system maintenance and repair
  • Building management system operation
  • Multi-trade basic competency
Transportation

Long-haul Truck Driver

Owner-operator or company driver. CDL-A, 11 hours/day on the road managing a 40-ton vehicle, load compliance, HOS logs, and DOT inspections. As freight automation grows, experienced drivers are looking for land-based alternatives.

  • Driving and operating Class 8 vehicles (80,000+ lbs GVW)
  • Pre-trip & post-trip DOT inspection routines
  • HOS log compliance via ELD systems
  • Load securing and weight distribution
  • Route planning and logistics coordination
