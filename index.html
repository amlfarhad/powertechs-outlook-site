The Challenge

Why Workforce Planning Fails

Traditional approaches overwhelm employers with data they can't act on.

Too Many Tasks

Hundreds of thousands of individual job tasks. A list of 50 new ones is overwhelming and useless to a hiring manager.

Too Far Away

Employers don't hire for 2035. They need to know who can start next month and what training bridges the gap.

Too Broad

A solar installer and an offshore driller both work in "energy" but share almost zero skills. Labels are meaningless.

Wrong Language

HR thinks in certifications, licenses, and union cards — not abstract "competencies" from academic frameworks.
